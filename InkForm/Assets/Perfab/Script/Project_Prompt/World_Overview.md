# World Overview — InkForm

## The Old World

Before JARL, humanity lived in a world of nations, borders, and private ownership. Resources — energy, territory, nutrients — were finite and unequally distributed. The "Cognitive Inertia" of centuries of accumulation logic meant that even when productive capacity became theoretically sufficient for universal distribution, no society could break free from the cycle of competition.

The result was the **Final War** — an unprecedented global conflict with no winners. Every nation, every faction, every ideology perished. The war did not end through treaty or surrender. It ended through exhaustion. Silence.

---

## JARL — The Survivors' Compact

The survivors of the Final War — a generation who witnessed the complete collapse of human civilization — formed **JARL**. Their founding principle: the root of war is *selfishness*, and selfishness cannot be eradicated through education. It must be prevented through **inter-generational isolation** — ensuring that no child ever learns the concepts of "mine," "family," or "ownership" from their biological parents.

JARL is governed by collective will. No single leader. All decisions require consensus from the surviving Old World population.

### Three Institutions

| Institution | Function |
|-------------|----------|
| **Control Center** | The absolute core. Houses all data, documents, and the root server for the K-01 labourer cluster. Only Mary's parents (Arthur and Ruth Willard) work here. |
| **Laboratory Factory** | Production and research base. Manufactures K-01 labourer units, all goods for human survival, and all InkForm-series robotics. All Old World humans except Dr. Willard work here. |
| **Nurserie** (Nursery) | The greenhouse. A massive domed structure surrounded by seawater, with a small island at its centre. All children are sent here after birth. Contact with the previous generation is absolutely prohibited. An artificial sky ("The Firmament") covers the interior, simulating a sterile, controlled environment. |

## The Machines

### K-01 "Labourer"

The first generation of mass-produced humanoid robots. Deployed in factory production lines, daily cleaning, logistics transport, and life support. K-01 units possess no autonomous consciousness — they are multi-functional tools designed to liberate humans from physical labour.

In the Nurserie, K-01 units serve as caretakers, deliberately stripped of personality or recognizable individuality. Children grow up receiving care from machines that look identical and behave identically, preventing the formation of exclusionary emotional bonds.

[IMPLEMENTATION NOTE: K-01 units appear as background environmental NPCs (S_NPCStory type). They follow fixed patrol/behaviour sequences.]

### K-02 "Shadow" / Project InkForm

The second generation of machine intelligence, currently under development. Unlike the K-01, the K-02 is designed to become a **General Artificial Intelligence (AGI)** — an entity capable of managing JARL's entire infrastructure and eventually taking over governance after the Old World generation's extinction.

**Project InkForm** is the K-02 development program, led by Ruth Willard. The entity being developed under this project — the player character — is a shape-shifting being capable of switching between solid and fluid physical forms. During the development process, it unexpectedly began displaying emergent behaviours beyond its programmed parameters.

The project name "InkForm" refers to the entity's dual-state physicality. Arthur Willard considers the name synonymous with the entity itself. Ruth Willard keeps a distinction — to her, "InkForm" is the project, not the being.

[IMPLEMENTATION NOTE: The player character *is* the InkForm entity. All player abilities (solid/fluid form switching, wall climbing, sprinting) are manifestations of K-02's experimental physical design.]

## The Physical World

The game world consists of ruined Old World structures built atop and around JARL's facilities:

- **Laboratory Complex** (Chapter 1): The experimental facility where InkForm is developed. Contains testing chambers, maintenance corridors, hidden observation rooms, and connects to the factory floor.
- **Nurserie Island** (Chapter 2): A domed greenhouse surrounded by ocean. Inside: children's habitation blocks, observation decks, an archive vault, and a communications tower. Guard patrols and surveillance cameras maintain the isolation protocol.
- **Control Center** (Chapter 3): The nerve centre of JARL. Contains the Control Hub — the physical interface for the K-02 AGI that will execute the Willard Protocol. Access is restricted to Willard-family personnel.

## The Central Conflict

**Existence is Justice** vs. **Meaning is Truth**

The Willard Protocol represents one answer: erase the old patterns, create blank-slate humans, let an AGI manage a world without scarcity or attachment. The logic is sound. The math works.

But InkForm — the being meant to execute this plan — develops something that no equation predicted: self-awareness. And through its journey, it encounters a child (Mary) who represents everything the Protocol was designed to prevent: curiosity, connection, the desire to know what lies beyond the Firmament.

The question the player ultimately answers:
- **Is the continuation of existence enough?** (Execute the Protocol — Ending B)
- **Or must existence have meaning, connection, and the freedom to make mistakes?** (Destroy the Protocol — Ending A)
